The Ukranian Legend

Here are some landmarks of our legend's Life:

  • 1976 - Born in Dvirkivshchyna, Ukrainian SSR on 29 September 1976.
  • 1984 - Moved to the newly built neighbourhood in Kyiv – Obolon.
  • 1986 - After Chernobyl disaster he was evacuated temporarily from the city electing to go on with Football after being a competitive boxer.
  • 1990 - Participated in the Ian Rush Cup (now the Welsh Super Cup) while playing for Dynamo Kieve U-14; he finished as the tournament's top scorer and was awarded a pair of Rush's boots as a prize by the then-Liverpool player.
  • 1993-1994 - at the second tier, Shevchenko was the top goal scorer for Dynamo-2 with 12 goals.
  • 1994-1995 - made his Vyshcha Liha debut for Dynamo squad on 8 November 1994 in an away game against Shakhtar Donetsk when he was 18.
  • 1997-1998 - Scored a hat-trick during an away UEFA Champions League game against Barcelona in the Camp Nou
  • 1999 - Joined Italian giants AC Milan for a then-record transfer fee of $25 million and managed to win Seria A top goalscorer in his debut season.
  • 2003 - Won The Uefa Champions League with AC Milan.
  • 2003-2004 - Scored the winning goal in the UEFA Super Cup against Porto and at the end of the season finished top goalscorer in Serie A and Won the italian League. Shevchenko married American model Kristen Pazik on 14 July 2004 in a private ceremony on a golf course in Washington, D.C.
  • 2004-2005 - Scored a hattrick in the italian Supercup agains Lazio and went on to win the Ballon D'or. However lost int the UEFA Champions League finals in a dramatic fashion in one of the most memorable games of all time in penalty shoot outs.
  • 2006 - On 8 February 2006, Shevchenko became Milan's second highest all-time goalscorer, behind Gunnar Nordahl. On 28 May 2006, Shevchenko left Milan for Chelsea for £30.8 million breaking the record for a player signed by an English club. He also scored six goals in qualifying for the 2006 World Cup, to take his country to its first ever major tournament.
  • 2008-2009 - Was bas on a season loan to AC Milan.
  • 2009-2010 - Shevchenko signed a two-year deal at his former club Dynamo Kyiv.
  • 2012 - Participated With Ukraine National team in Euro 2012 scoring 2 goals and on 28 July 2012, Shevchenko announced that he was quitting football for politics.
  • 2016 - From 16 February to 15 July 2016, Shevchenko served as the assistant manager of the Ukraine national team. On 15 July 2016, Shevchenko was appointed as manager of the Ukraine national team.
  • 2021 - At UEFA Euro 2020, Shevchenko led Ukraine to reach the quarter-finals of the European Championship for the first time in their history. On 1 August 2021, he announced his departure from the post

During his illustrious career Shevchenko played 704 senior squad matchs scoring 343 goals, and represented Ukraine in 111 matches scoring 48 goals.
